Synthesis Which to pick

A is the most consistent. If single-chart and two-chart panels live on the same page, the eye doesn't have to switch hierarchies. The trade is one extra ~24 px row of header chrome per single-chart panel.

B is the most compact. Strong choice on pages with several small single-chart panels (e.g. a memory page with multiple per-bucket charts) where vertical room is scarce and the chip already conveys range + granularity.

C is the most informative on one line. Works when there's exactly one chart per page and the title pulls double duty. Risky if the title text grows or localizes.
