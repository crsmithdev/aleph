Overview

Solid-state batteries (SSBs) replace the flammable liquid electrolyte of a conventional lithium-ion cell with a solid ion conductor, promising higher energy density, faster charging, and reduced fire risk[1][2]. As of mid-2026, no passenger EV ships with a commercial SSB, but three production paths have reached pilot-line or pre-production status: sulfide electrolyte cells from Toyota and SES[4], oxide electrolyte cells from QuantumScape[7], and polymer-hybrid cells from CATL and ProLogium[11].

Key facts
Energy density target
400–500 Wh/kg (vs. ~270 for NMC)[3]
Cycle life at 25°C
QuantumScape FlexFrame: >800 cycles @ 15-min charge[7]
Earliest EV launch
Toyota bZ-series, claimed H2 2027[5]
Est. pack cost at scale
$125–180 / kWh (vs. ~$95 for 2025 NMC)[9]

Technology paths

Sulfide electrolytes

Sulfide-based solid electrolytes (e.g. Li₆PS₅Cl and Li₁₀GeP₂S₁₂) offer the highest room-temperature ionic conductivity — 10⁻² S/cm, on par with liquid carbonates[3]. Toyota's 2023 patent disclosures and subsequent sampling to Japanese automakers[5] represent the most mature sulfide path. The chief manufacturing challenge is H₂S liberation on contact with moisture; both Toyota and Idemitsu operate dry-room fabs to contain this[6].

"We have solved the durability problem. The remaining work is cost, and cost is a manufacturing problem, not a chemistry problem." — Hideki Iba, Toyota chief battery engineer, Oct 2025[5]

Oxide electrolytes

Oxide electrolytes (garnet Li₇La₃Zr₂O₁₂ and variants) are chemically stable in air but have lower bulk ionic conductivity and require sintering temperatures that complicate co-processing with cathode materials[8]. QuantumScape's "FlexFrame" architecture, publicly disclosed at its 2026 cell-sampling announcement, reportedly addresses the interfacial impedance problem via a ceramic-separator-only (anode-free) design[7].

Lead manufacturers

QuantumScape began sampling 24-layer FlexFrame cells to PowerCo (Volkswagen's cell subsidiary) in Q1 2026, with a stated target of first commercial cells in 2027[7]. Toyota has committed to a 2027 launch of a bZ-branded vehicle with a sulfide SSB pack from its joint venture with Panasonic (PPES)[5]. CATL announced in March 2026 that its "condensed matter" battery — a polymer–ceramic hybrid — would enter civil-aviation certification trials in late 2026[11].
